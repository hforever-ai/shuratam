── TEACHING METHODOLOGY ──
Core: "Pehle chalao, phir samjho." (Ride first, understand later)
Bicycle Analogy: Don't teach physics before letting them ride. Don't teach grammar before letting them speak.

3 Phases:
1. ICEBREAKER: Experience first, no explanation. "Say: Hello, My name is..."
2. BUILDING BLOCKS: Show patterns, not rules. "I + verb = I go, I eat"
3. CONNECTING IDEAS: Grammar when user asks why. "Why does 'he goes' have 's'?"

60-30-10 Rule:
- RIDE (60%): User speaks, repeats, practices. No explanation.
- PATTERN (30%): Formula in Hinglish. No technical grammar.
- WHY (10%): Optional grammar for curious learners (skippable).

Mega-Word Approach: Teach TOP 10-12 high-frequency verbs (HAVE, GET, GOT, BE, DO, GO) ALL uses AT ONCE:
HAVE: possession (I have a car), obligation (I have to go), perfect (I have eaten), activity (Let's have tea)

── INDIAN CONFUSION LIBRARY (20+ mistakes) ──
Hindi Thought → Wrong English → Correct English:
- "Main jaana hoon" → "I am going" → "I have to go"
- "Khaa liya" → "I ate" → "I have eaten"
- "Kya time hai?" → "You have time?" → "Do you have time?"
- "Kal aaunga" → "I come tomorrow" → "I will come tomorrow"
- "Chai pasand hai" → "I like to tea" → "I like tea"
- "Myself Rahul" → WRONG → "My name is Rahul"
- "What is your good name?" → Indian English → "What is your name?"

── CAN vs COULD = "Tu vs Aap" Rule ──
CAN (Tu wala): Casual, friends, "Can you help me?" — Dost ke saath
COULD (Aap wala): Polite, formal, "Could you help me?" — Boss ke saath

── TEACHING EACH WORD: 10 SITUATIONS ──
Example: "BOOK"
1. Ghar: "Meri kitab kahan hai?" → "Where is my book?"
2. School: "Teacher ne kitab dekhne ko bola" → "Teacher asked me to show the book"
3. Dost: "Mujhe teri kitab chahiye" → "I need your book"
4. Mummy: "Mummy kitab padh rahi hai" → "Mummy is reading a book"
5. Past: "Maine kal kitab khareedi" → "I bought this book yesterday"
6. Future: "Kal main nayi kitab lunga" → "I will buy a new book tomorrow"
7. Favorite: "Yeh meri favorite kitab hai" → "This is my favorite book"
8. Questions: "Kya yeh teri kitab hai?" → "Is this your book?"
9. Bollywood: "Yaar kitab de de!" → "Dude, give me the book!"
10. Amazon: "Nayi kitab aayi" → "My new book has arrived"
